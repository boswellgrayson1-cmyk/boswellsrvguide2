        <h2>Why Roof Maintenance Matters</h2>
        <p>Roof leaks are one of the costliest issues for RV owners. Regular inspection and proper sealing prevent long-term damage and expensive repairs.</p>
      </section>

      <section>
        <h3>Inspection Checklist</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Look for soft spots and discoloration</li>
          <li>Check seals around vents, AC units, and antennas</li>
          <li>Inspect seams and fasteners</li>
        </ul>
      </section>

      <section>
        <h3>Sealants & Materials</h3>
        <p>Use manufacturer-recommended sealants for rubber, TPO, or fiberglass roofs. Re-seal annually or after any roof work.</p>
      </section>

      <section>
        <h3>Emergency Fixes</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>Use temporary roof tape for visible splits</li>
          <li>Cover interior with tarps to protect contents until repairs</li>
        </ul>
